"album": A published collection of musical tracks grouped as a single artistic release.
"award": A formal recognition granted for excellence in musical performance, composition, or production.
"band": A group of musicians performing together under a shared name, often creating and recording collective works.
"country": A nation or territory associated with the origin, influence, or identity of musicians, bands, or music styles.
"event": A public or organized occurrence involving music, performances, or artistic gatherings, often linked to festivals or competitions.
"location": A geographic or cultural place where musical activities occur, including cities, venues, or performance halls.
"musical artist": A performer, singer, composer, or instrumentalist recognized for their creative or interpretive contribution to music.
"musical instrument": A device designed to produce musical sounds used in composition, performance, or recording.
"music genre": A category of music distinguished by stylistic, cultural, or rhythmic characteristics.
"organisation": A company, association, or group involved in the creation, distribution, or promotion of music.
"person": An individual mentioned in a musical context who is not necessarily a performer, such as producers, designers, or other figures related to the industry.
"song": A distinct musical composition featuring lyrics or instrumental arrangements released or performed as an individual piece.
