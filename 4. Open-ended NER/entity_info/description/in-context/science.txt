"academic journal": A periodical publication dedicated to presenting peer-reviewed scientific research and findings.
"astronomical object": A natural entity in space, including planets, stars, or other celestial bodies observed or studied in astronomy.
"award": A distinction or honor granted for notable contributions or achievements in scientific or academic fields.
"chemical compound": A substance formed from two or more elements chemically bonded in fixed proportions, often used or studied in scientific research.
"chemical element": A pure substance consisting of atoms with the same number of protons, forming the building blocks of matter.
"country": A nation or territory associated with scientific research, institutions, or discoveries.
"discipline": A specialized branch or field of scientific study or academic inquiry.
"enzyme": A biological catalyst that accelerates chemical reactions within living organisms.
"event": A gathering, competition, or conference related to scientific activity, presentation, or recognition.
"location": A geographic or institutional place relevant to scientific research, discovery, or activity.
"organisation": An institution, association, or laboratory involved in scientific research, healthcare, or education.
"person": An individual referenced in a scientific context who is not explicitly identified as a scientist.
"protein": A biological macromolecule composed of amino acids, essential for structural, catalytic, or regulatory functions in living organisms.
"scientist": A person engaged in systematic study or experimentation to advance knowledge in scientific fields.
"theory": A conceptual framework or explanatory model developed to describe, predict, or understand scientific phenomena.
"university": An academic or research institution engaged in teaching or advancing scientific knowledge.
