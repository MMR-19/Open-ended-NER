"academic journal": A periodical publication that disseminates peer-reviewed scientific or scholarly research.
"astronomical object": A physical entity existing in space and studied by astronomy.
"award": A formal recognition given for notable scientific achievements or contributions.
"chemical compound": A substance formed by the chemical combination of two or more elements in fixed proportions.
"chemical element": A pure substance consisting of one type of atom, defined by its atomic number.
"country": A sovereign state often mentioned in the context of scientific institutions, research, or collaborations.
"discipline": A distinct branch of scientific study or research area.
"enzyme": A biological molecule that catalyzes biochemical reactions.
"event": A formal scientific gathering, conference, or occasion relevant to research dissemination.
"location": A geographical place where scientific activities, discoveries, or institutions are situated.
"organisation": An institution, agency, or company involved in scientific research or education.
"person": An individual referred to in the context of scientific activity or authorship.
"protein": A biological macromolecule composed of amino acids with specific structural or functional roles.
"scientist": A person professionally engaged in scientific investigation or research.
"theory": A systematic framework or set of principles that explains phenomena within a scientific field.
"university": A higher education and research institution involved in scientific studies or collaborations.
